- 4 cloves garlic, minced

- 1 large carrot, julienned

- 1 tsp salt

**For the Sandwich:**
- Crusty bread rolls
- Lettuce leaves
- Tomato slices
- Mayonnaise (optional)

   - Coat the pork shoulder with the rub and let it marinate for at least 1 hour, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
   - Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C).
   - In a roasting pan, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Brown the pork on all sides.
   - Add the stock to the pan, cover with aluminum foil or a lid, and place it in the oven.
   - Roast for about 3 hours, or until the pork is tender and shreds easily with a fork.

3. **Assemble the Sandwich:**

   - Add lettuce and tomato slices.
   - Top with a good amount of pickled vegetables.
   - If you like it spicy, add llajwa or your favorite hot sauce.
   - Place the top half of the roll on the sandwich and press down slightly.

4. **Serve:**
   - The Chola Sandwich is best served immediately after assembling, while the bread is still crispy and the pork is warm.
